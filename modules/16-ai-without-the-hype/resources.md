# 📚 Resources 16 — Go Deeper on AI, Calmly

All free. Ordered roughly from "play right now" to "settle in for a course."

## 🖱️ Play (the fastest way to *get* it)

- **Teachable Machine — teachablemachine.withgoogle.com** — train a real
  image/sound/pose model in your browser in five minutes, no account. The single best
  way to *feel* what training data means. Best for: everyone; it's the heart of this
  module's lab.
- **Quick, Draw! — quickdraw.withgoogle.com** — a neural network guesses your 20-second
  doodles, then shows you the thousands of drawings it learned from. Best for: seeing
  training data with your own eyes, and laughing.

## 🎬 Watch

- **3Blue1Brown — Neural Networks series (YouTube — search "3Blue1Brown neural
  networks")** — the gold-standard visual explanation of what a neural network actually
  is, with the most beautiful animations in math education. Honest note: it gets
  genuinely mathy from the middle of the series onward — **watch episode 1**, which is
  approachable and wonderful, and treat the rest as optional. Best for: anyone who
  wants one level deeper than this module.
- **Crash Course Artificial Intelligence (YouTube — the "Crash Course AI" series)** —
  a friendly, well-produced tour of AI topics (learning, neural nets, bias, language)
  in short episodes, no math required. Best for: bingeable breadth after this module.

## 📖 Take a gentle course

- **Elements of AI — elementsofai.com** — the famous free course from the University of
  Helsinki, built to teach *the general public* what AI is and isn't. Plain language,
  small exercises, a certificate at the end. Hundreds of thousands of ordinary people
  have finished it. Best for: the natural next step if this module left you hungry.
- **Google's free AI introductions (search "Google AI essentials" or visit
  ai.google)** — Google maintains free, non-technical introductions to AI concepts and
  to using AI tools well at work. Best for: practical, work-focused follow-up.

## 🧭 How to use these

Play with Teachable Machine and Quick, Draw! first — twenty minutes, no reading. Then
watch 3Blue1Brown episode 1 the next evening. If the spark catches, Elements of AI is
your next month of enjoyable Sunday mornings.
