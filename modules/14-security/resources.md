# 📚 Resources 14 — Go Deeper on Security

All free. The first two aren't reading — they're tools you'll actually reuse for
years.

## 🧪 Tools worth bookmarking

- **[Have I Been Pwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com)** — the breach-checking service
  from the lab, run by a respected security professional; you can also subscribe to
  get notified if your email appears in *future* breaches. Best as a set-and-forget
  early-warning system.
- **[Google Phishing Quiz](https://phishingquiz.withgoogle.com)** — the
  spot-the-fake training from the lab; the examples rotate and stay current. Best
  re-taken yearly, and shared with the family member most likely to click things.

## 📖 Reading

- **[EFF — Surveillance Self-Defense](https://ssd.eff.org)** — plain-language
  security guides from the Electronic Frontier Foundation, a decades-old digital
  rights nonprofit: passwords, 2FA, encrypted messaging, and tailored guides for
  journalists, activists, and ordinary humans. Best next step after this module —
  the same honest tone, more depth.
- **[Khan Academy — Online Data Security](https://www.khanacademy.org/computing/computers-and-internet)** —
  the "Online data security" unit walks through attacks, encryption, and defenses
  with exercises and self-checks. Best for learners who want quiz-as-you-go
  structure.

## 🎬 Videos

- **[Crash Course Computer Science — cybersecurity episodes](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNlUrzyH5r6jN9ulIgZBpdo)** —
  episodes 31–33 ("Cybersecurity", "Hackers & Cyber Attacks", "Cryptography")
  animate this module's ideas, including a friendly tour of the padlock-mailbox
  math. Best for a visual replay of encryption and attacks in ~36 total minutes.
