# 📚 Resources 11 — Go Deeper on Algorithms & Data Structures

All free unless marked otherwise. None are required — pick whatever matches how you
like to learn.

## 🖱️ Interactive

- **[VisuAlgo](https://visualgo.net)** — beautiful, click-to-play animations of
  sorting, searching, trees, graphs, and more, built by a university team. Best for
  visual learners who want to *watch* the recipes from this module run step by step.
- **[University of San Francisco — Data Structure Visualizations](https://www.cs.usfca.edu/~galles/visualization/Algorithms.html)** —
  a classic, plainer set of animations covering nearly every structure in this
  module. Best for slowly stepping through one operation at a time at your own pace.

## 🎬 Videos

- **[CS50's algorithms lecture](https://cs50.harvard.edu/x/)** — Harvard's famous
  free intro course; the week on algorithms includes the legendary live phone-book
  demonstration. Best for people who enjoy an energetic lecture and don't mind ~1
  hour. (Find the "Algorithms" week on the course page — no sign-up needed to watch.)
- **[Crash Course Computer Science — "Intro to Algorithms" and "Data Structures"](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNlUrzyH5r6jN9ulIgZBpdo)** —
  episodes 13 and 14 of a fast, friendly animated series. Best for a 12-minute
  refresher of this whole module.
- **[AlgoRythmics — sorting algorithms as folk dances](https://www.youtube.com/user/AlgoRythmics)** —
  a Hungarian university troupe performs bubble sort and merge sort as actual folk
  dances. Genuinely famous, genuinely delightful. Best for anyone who thinks they
  "don't get" sorting — you will after watching people dance it.

## 📖 Reading

- **Grokking Algorithms** by Aditya Bhargava *(paid book, optional)* — widely
  considered the friendliest algorithms book ever written: hand-drawn pictures,
  everyday examples, minimal math. Best for readers who finished this module hungry
  for one gentle step further. Your local library may have it.
