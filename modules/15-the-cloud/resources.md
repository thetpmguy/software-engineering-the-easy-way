# 📚 Resources 15 — Go Deeper on the Cloud

All free. Pick what matches your curiosity — you don't need all of them.

## 🎬 Watch

- **Google Data Center tour videos (YouTube — search "Google data center tour")** —
  Google's official walk-throughs of its data centers: server aisles, cooling systems,
  hard-drive shredders. The single best way to make the cloud feel physical. Best for:
  everyone; start here.
- **AWS data center / infrastructure videos (YouTube — search "AWS data center")** —
  Amazon's official looks inside its infrastructure, including how regions and
  redundancy work. Best for: seeing how the biggest landlord runs its buildings.
- **Crash Course Computer Science (YouTube — the "Crash Course Computer Science"
  series)** — the episodes on the internet, the web, and cloud-era computing tie this
  module back to Modules 01–03 with lovely animations. Best for: visual learners who
  enjoyed those earlier modules.

## 🖱️ Explore interactively

- **Submarine Cable Map — submarinecablemap.com** — a live, zoomable map of the
  undersea cables that carry the internet (and your cloud data) between continents.
  Find the cables serving your country; suddenly latency makes visceral sense. Best
  for: the "wait, the internet is *wires under the ocean*?" moment.
- **AWS Pricing Calculator — calculator.aws** — the official menu of the world's
  biggest computer landlord, no account needed. Price servers, storage, and more, and
  watch how each choice moves the bill. Best for: feeling why cloud costs are a
  profession.

## 📖 Read

- **Cloudflare Learning Center — cloudflare.com/learning** — short, genuinely
  plain-English articles on "What is the cloud?", "What is a CDN?", "What is
  serverless?" — written by a company that runs a huge chunk of the internet's plumbing.
  Best for: a trustworthy second explanation of anything in this module.
- **Explainer journalism on "how the cloud works" (search Vox or The Wall Street
  Journal + "data center")** — mainstream outlets have produced excellent illustrated
  pieces and videos on data centers, their locations, and their energy and water use.
  Best for: the environmental and economic side of the story, told for a general
  audience.

## 🧭 How to use these

Watch a data center tour first (10 minutes, makes everything concrete). Then spend a
curious evening on the submarine cable map. Keep Cloudflare's Learning Center bookmarked
as your "explain that cloud word again?" dictionary.
