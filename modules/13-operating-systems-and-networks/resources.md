# 📚 Resources 13 — Go Deeper on Operating Systems & Networks

All free, all reputable, none required. The first two are the gentlest.

## 🎬 Videos

- **[Crash Course Computer Science — "Operating Systems" (episode 18)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNlUrzyH5r6jN9ulIgZBpdo)** —
  a fast, funny 12-minute animation of scheduling, memory, and the kernel; the whole
  playlist's networking episodes (28–31) continue into Part B territory. Best for a
  visual replay of this entire module.
- **["How does the Internet work?" by code.org](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLzdnOPI1iJNfMRZm5DDxco3UdsFegvuB7)** —
  a short, famously clear video series featuring internet pioneers explaining
  packets, IP addresses, and DNS with real props. Best for anyone who wants Module
  03 + this module's Part B told by the people who built it.

## 📖 Interactive & articles

- **[Khan Academy — Computers and the Internet](https://www.khanacademy.org/computing/computers-and-internet)** —
  patient lessons with self-check questions covering hardware, the internet, and
  online data. Best for learners who like reading at their own pace with quizzes
  along the way.
- **[Cloudflare Learning Center](https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/)** — plain-
  English explainer articles on DNS, VPNs, firewalls, latency, and more, written by
  a company that runs a large chunk of the internet's plumbing. Best as a lookup
  shelf whenever a networking word appears in the news.

## 🧪 Tools you'll actually reuse

- **[fast.com](https://fast.com)** — a one-button speed test run by Netflix; click
  "Show more info" for latency. Best for a ten-second check with zero clutter.
- **[speedtest.net](https://www.speedtest.net)** — the detailed classic: download,
  upload, and ping, with history if you want it. Best when you're gathering evidence
  for a call to your internet provider.
